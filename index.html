Welcome to the World of Moroccan Calligraphy

Moroccan calligraphy is more than just beautiful writing. it is a profound expression of culture, spirituality, and artistry. For centuries, it has adorned mosques, manuscripts, and monuments, leaving an indelible mark on the world of art.

- History of Moroccan calligraphy -

Arabic script entered the Maghreb countries in the first century AH/7 AD, that is, with the Islamic conquest of Morocco, and all writing styles from the East were adopted, including kufic script, as mentioned in the city of Kairouan, the capital of the region, and from there it spread to the rest of the Maghreb. The Moroccan script is a direct descendant of the kufic script, so it was derived from Levantine scripts before applying the calligraphic reforms introduced by Ibn Muqla in what is known as the attributed script.

Types

Moroccan calligraphy evolved into different types of scripts the most know ones are the Mabsout, the Moroccan Thuluth also know as Maghrebised Mashreqi script and the Mujawhar.

Modern Era

Today, Arabic calligraphy continues to thrive, blending traditional techniques with modern art. It is used in everything from religious texts to contemporary design and architecture.

- Styles of Moroccan calligraphy -

Moroccan calligraphy encompasses a variety of styles, each with its own unique characteristics and history. Below are some of the most prominent styles:

Kufic

Kufic is the oldest form of Moroccan calligraphy, characterized by its angular and geometric shapes. It was widely used in early Quranic manuscripts and architectural inscriptions.

Moroccan Thuluth

Thuluth is known for its elegance, decorative nature and cursive writing. It is often used in architectural decorations and religious texts.

Mabsout

Mabsout is prominent for its simple writing and clarity.It is well-known for its use in writing the Quran.

Mujawhar

Mujawhar is known for its small writing. Royalty frequently employed it to compose significant documents, including secret letters or records.It was invented during the era of the Saadiyin.
